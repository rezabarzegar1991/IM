The Core Problem

The Lisbon Housing Crisis Isn't What You Think It Is.

We all see the symptoms: rising rents, endless competition, and a feeling that locals are being pushed out. But our research identified a Core Structural Failure:

The Financialization-Induced Decoupling of Housing Costs from Local Wages.

The system has transformed housing from a social necessity into a global, leveraged asset. This single "decoupling" is the source of the problem.

Who Is Left Behind?

When the System Decouples, It Excludes.

This isn't an abstract problem. It has a human cost, systematically failing key groups.

Our North Star

We Can Redesign the System.

Our goal isn't just to add more houses; it's to change the connections of the system itself. This is our Shared Future Vision.

Current State Future Vision

Current State: Housing is a Global Asset

The market is driven by speculation, and governance is a slow, bureaucratic bottleneck.

Future Vision: Housing is Social Infrastructure

Markets are regulated to serve local wages, speculation is curbed, and governance is a fast, transparent, digital enabler.

To make those changes, we must deactivate the inhibitors and activate the enablers.

Obstacles (to deactivate)

  • Vested Interest Protection (Lobbying)
  • Governance Failure (Bureaucratic Bottlenecks)
  • Foundational Legal Bias (Property > People)
  • Policy/Financial Bias (Exclusion)

Opportunities (to activate)

  • Reclaiming Sovereignty (Taxes, STR Controls)
  • Regulatory Acceleration (Digital Permits)
  • Inclusive Credit Systems (New Bonds)
  • Incentivizing Use (Activating Vacant Homes)

Level 3: Collaborative Actions (The "Experiments")

We start with small, collaborative experiments that "change the system's connections."
